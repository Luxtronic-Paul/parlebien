The Science

Proven, not trendy

ParleBien isn't built around streaks and gamification. It's built around three learning mechanisms that have been validated by over a century of research.

01

Spaced Repetition

Reviewing material at increasing intervals exploits the spacing effect — the finding that memory is stronger when learning is distributed over time rather than crammed. First documented by Hermann Ebbinghaus in 1885. Still the most powerful tool in learning science.

Ebbinghaus, 1885
02

Active Recall

Being required to produce an answer from memory — rather than recognise it from a list — creates 2–3× stronger retention than passive reading or multiple choice. Tapping a word teaches you to tap a word. Speaking it teaches you to speak it.

2–3× retention boost
03

Multisensory Encoding

When a word is seen, heard, and spoken simultaneously, three independent memory pathways activate in a single moment. Multi-modal encoding produces memories that are stronger, more accessible, and more resistant to forgetting.

3 memory pathways
